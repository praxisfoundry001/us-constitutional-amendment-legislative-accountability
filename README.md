# U.S. Constitutional Amendment: Legislative Accountability

## What is this?
A public-use constitutional amendment artifact designed to compel recorded floor consideration of introduced legislation without dictating legislative outcomes.

This is a printable civic artifact. It is meant to be read, shared, discussed, and handed to legislators, advocates, journalists, organizers, and institutions.

## Why should I care?
This amendment addresses a simple accountability problem:
Congress can allow legislation to die without a recorded floor vote, even when public support is real and sustained.

The amendment creates procedural release mechanisms that force consideration while preserving debate, amendment, rejection, and representative judgment.

## What is the claim?
The claim is constitutional and practical:

a republic can preserve legislative discretion while preventing persistent procedural burial of legislation by requiring a recorded vote once public or governmental threshold triggers are met.

## What is this not?
This artifact is not:
- a mandate to pass any bill
- a substitute for legislative judgment
- a constitutional amendment about substantive policy outcomes
- enacted law

It is a model constitutional amendment text focused on floor-vote accountability.

## How was this done?
The amendment creates procedural discharge mechanisms through:
- citizen petitions
- local-entity resolutions
- state-legislature resolutions

It preserves chamber procedure, legislative debate, amendment, and rejection while preventing indefinite suppression of recorded accountability.
